// STRESS TEST A — STEADY-STATE HEAT REJECTION BALANCE · WHERE DOES 106 kW ULTIMATELY GO?

The critical question: 106.4 kW of heat is removed from servers — but it must be permanently transferred to a thermodynamic sink at steady state. A system that works for hours but saturates its sinks over weeks is not a steady-state solution. Each sink has a finite capacity determined by physics, not design intent.

```

🌍 SINK 1 — GROUND LOOP

Heat flows from loop fluid (32°C) into soil (22–24°C). Capacity governed by Fourier conduction. See Stress Test B for saturation analysis.

// STEADY-STATE GROUND CAPACITY
Q_geo = U·A·(T_fluid − T_earth)
= 50 × 60 × (32−24)
= 24,000 W = 24 kW
// Valid only while T_earth stays ≤24°C
// Long-term: see soil diffusion model →
24 kW · Conditional on soil temp stability

💨 SINK 2 — ATMOSPHERE (EVAP + CHIMNEY)

Evaporation converts liquid water to vapour, carrying latent heat permanently into the atmosphere. This is a truly infinite sink — atmosphere has unlimited capacity. The constraint is evaporation rate, not atmospheric capacity.

// LATENT HEAT PATHWAY (permanent sink ✓)
Q_evap = ṁ_water × h_fg
h_fg(30°C) = 2,431 kJ/kg
At η=50% (RH=80%): Q = 41 kW
At η=85% (RH=55%): Q = 70 kW

// Sensible heat via chimney (also atmosphere)
Q_chimney = ṁ_air × Cp × ΔT
= 2.72 × 1006 × 5 = 13.7 kW
// ṁ_air = ρ × Q_v = 1.21 × 2.25 = 2.72 kg/s
Atmosphere = infinite sink ✓ Rate-limited only

🌧 SINK 3 — WATER MASS BALANCE

Water carries heat away via two paths: evaporation (latent) and blowdown (sensible). Malaysia's 3,000 L/day rainfall completely covers evaporation demand at typical load, making this sink effectively renewable.

// STRESS TEST B — GEOTHERMAL THERMAL SATURATION · FOURIER DIFFUSION · SOIL RECHARGE RATE

Injecting 24 kW continuously into a finite soil volume raises mean soil temperature over time. Without modelling this, geothermal "22–24°C stable" is only true at t=0. The question is: does T_soil approach T_fluid asymptotically (killing heat transfer) or does it reach a new stable equilibrium?

🌡 FOURIER SOIL HEAT DIFFUSION MODEL

// FOURIER'S LAW — RADIAL CONDUCTION
∂T/∂t = α · ∇²T
// α = k/(ρ·Cp) — thermal diffusivity

// MALAYSIA LATERITE SOIL PROPERTIES
k = 1.2 W/m·K (thermal conductivity)
ρ = 1,800 kg/m³ (density)
Cp = 840 J/kg·K (specific heat)
α = 1.2/(1800×840) = 7.94×10⁻⁷ m²/s

// TEMPERATURE RISE AT PIPE SURFACE
ΔT_pipe = Q/(2π·k·L) · ln(r_soil/r_pipe)
= 24000/(2π·1.2·60) · ln(5/0.025)
= 53.05 × 5.298 = +2.4°C at pipe surface
⟹ Pipe surface rises +2.4°C above far-field soil
```

📐 THERMAL INFLUENCE RADIUS

// RADIUS OF THERMAL INFLUENCE vs TIME
r_influence = √(4·α·t)

After 1 month (2.59×10⁶s):
r = √(4 × 7.94e-7 × 2.59e6) = 2.87 m

After 1 year (3.15×10⁷s):
r = √(4 × 7.94e-7 × 3.15e7) = 9.99 m ≈ 10 m

After 5 years:
r = √(4 × 7.94e-7 × 1.58e8) = 22.4 m

// Soil volume thermally influenced:
V(1yr) = π·r²·L = π×100×60 = 18,850 m³
⟹ 10m radius after 1 yr — stays within site boundary

📈 SOIL TEMPERATURE RISE OVER TIME

// MEAN SOIL TEMP RISE (simplified, uniform injection)
ΔT_mean(t) = Q·t / (ρ·Cp·V_soil)

V_soil (60m pipe, 5m depth, 2m spacing):
= 60 × 2 × 2 = 240 m³ immediate zone

Rate of rise: dT/dt = Q/(ρ·Cp·V)
= 24000/(1800×840×240)
= 6.61×10⁻⁵ °C/s = 0.24°C/day

// BUT: Malaysia night recharge reduces this

= 1.2×(22−24)/5 = −0.48 W/m²
Over 60m² loop area: −28.8 W recharge
// Net: 24,000 − 29 ≈ 23,971 W still injected
⟹ Night recharge is negligible — soil does heat up
